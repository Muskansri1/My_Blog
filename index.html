About Me

My name is Muskan Srivastava and I am a 4th year engineering student in Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra studying Computer Science and Engineering. 3 years of college and studying around 50 plus subjects, I have realized that life always throws choices at you. It's now on you to decide, whether you regret your choices or do something good with them.
Procrastinator at heart, I love to travel, be on stage, act, read novels, listen to music and attempt to sing. From a homesick girl in first year to a responsible and independent adult, my journey has been quite exhilarating.

My Fault

16.12.2013

“What good is ye world when ye canst not livest hither.” “The world which worships Mother Mary and goddess Durga also has experienced such heinous crimes against her daughters.”

Full Transcript:

MY FAULT I am not a sex object, 
Neither am I a loaf of bread,
Nor I am fragile like a flower,
And
Surely I was not deserved to be raped.
It's my choice of living,
Who are you to tell me what should I do,
My clothes, my friends, my time, my needs.
Why do I need to be protected from such cruel bastards?
Why can't there be a place where i need not to be protected.
And why are these monsters not supposed to die?
Why was I the one who died?
I, the one who suffered!
I, the one who wept, cried and faced.
Why should I face?
What have I done?
Was I an object whom you used for your enjoyment and excitement?
Was I a tissue that you threw me on the chilly road naked?
I was a human.
A human who was once happy, sad, laughing, struggling and most of all,alive!
There was a hope, after I died,
That people might change
But years after, all I got was a tight slap on my face.
Because I forgot, that Rape is a girl's fault.
So yes, It was my fault.
It was my fault that I was born as a girl.
It was my fault that I thought I might illuminate someone's life.
It was my fault that i thought I was a human,
Because I forgot, that I was just
A loaf of bread for dog's to attack,
A fragile flower to be plucked,
And a sex object for sick monsters like you.
Yes, It was my fault.

Trust

He who does not trust enough will not be trusted." --Lao Tzu

Full Transcript:

All i am asking you is to trust me,
Trust me enough to let me fall,
Trust me enough to let me stand back on my toes.
I will make mistakes, we both are aware of that
But trust me enough to rectify them myself
Trust me enough to not stop me before i do anything stupid.
Can you do that for me?
All i am asking you is to trust me
Trust me enough to let me go
Trust me enough to wait for my return
I wamt to be free from the things that cage me
We know that i might get lost
But trust me enough that i will find my own way back
Can you do that for me?
All i am asking you is to trust this trust
The trust that i have for you
The trust that binds us close
There would be a lot of ups and downs
We know we might fall apart
But trust our trust enough that we will build ourselves back
Can you do that for me?

To Dear Mom

04.03.2017

“Youth fades; love droops; the leaves of friendship fall; A mother’s secret hope outlives them all.”

Full Transcript:

The game of hide and seek, the unlimited laughing riot
The book store visits, the spicy young gossip
Some memories I wish I could clone
Walking on the streets alone
All the love, the warmth she spread
On that brown whole wheat bread
She was not just a mom, but a friend of mine
But oh! By the looks, she looked just so fine
Leaving her was such a dreadful task
That pretentiously excited mask which couldn't even last
She knew all of me, hiding from her was never my cup of tea
Whether it was a game of hide and seek
Or it was my broken heart
She knew she could handle it with some long drive, coffee and a filled shopping cart
Late night studies, to heart felt 2 a.m. conversations
Umm, well she was never by my side, 'cause she needed her beauty relaxation
You see she was not a night person
But oh! Rise and shine that beautiful face
Ever so young and joyful face
The mornings that she loved the most
Where have those days gone lost?
Just some memories I wish I could clone
Walking on these streets alone
